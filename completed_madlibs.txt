I dwell in a SKINNY house I know That EFFECT many a summer ago, And left no DIFFERENCE but the cellar walls, And a cellar in which the daylight falls And the purple-stemmed wild raspberries grow. O'er ruined fences the grape-vines shield The woods come back to the FAST field; The SUCCESSFULLY tree has grown one copse Of new wood and old where the DISEASE chops; The footpath down to the well is healed. I dwell with a strangely aching MOM In that vanished abode there far apart On that QUIRKY and forgotten road That as no MOM now for the toad. Night comes; the black bats FEED and dart; The TOOTH is coming to shout And hush and CONCLUDE and flutter about: I hear him begin far enough away Full many a time to say his say Before he arrives to say it out. It is under the small, GOOFY, summer star. I know not who these mute folk are Who share the CRABBY place with me— Those stones out under the low-limbed tree Doubtless bear names that the mosses mar. They are DIVERGENT folk, but slow and sad— Though two, close-keeping, are lass and ad,— With none among them that ever PLEASE, And yet, in view of how many things, As sweet SILKY as might be had.
